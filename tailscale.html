Home Internet Anywhere

This guide explains how to turn your home router into a secure gateway, allowing your mobile devices to use your home IP address from any Wi-Fi or cellular network in the world.

Key Benefit: Securely access your home NAS or cameras while making your travel device appear as if it is still at home.
Phase 1

The Home Gateway (Router)

Your router acts as the "Exit Node." This is the point where your remote traffic enters the internet.

  • Enable Tailscale in your router settings (common in GL.iNet or OpenWrt devices).
  • In the Tailscale Admin Console, select your router and enable "Exit Node".
  • Toggle "Subnet Routing" if you want to see other local devices (Printers, NAS).
Screen: Router UI with Tailscale Exit Node enabled
Phase 2

Remote Viewing (Android TV)

Perfect for traveling with an Android TV stick. By connecting to your home exit node, you can bypass streaming "home location" restrictions.

  1. Install Tailscale from the Play Store on your Android TV.
  2. Log in and select your Home Router from the "Exit Node" menu.
Screen: Android TV Tailscale UI selecting Exit Node
Phase 3

Mobile Security (Android Phone)

Encrypt your mobile traffic and use your home IP on public Wi-Fi.

  • Open the Tailscale app on your phone.
  • Tap the three dots (menu) and select "Use Exit Node".
  • Choose your home router to route all traffic through your home connection.
Important: Android limits you to one active VPN. If you use Tailscale as an Exit Node, you cannot simultaneously run another VPN app like ProtonVPN.
